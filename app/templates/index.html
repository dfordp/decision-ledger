Welcome to DecisionLedger

An AI-powered proof of concept that demonstrates how vendors can leverage historical memory, policy enforcement, and semantic reasoning to evaluate new tender opportunities.

🧠 Historical Memory

DecisionLedger stores past proposals and decisions with semantic embeddings, enabling intelligent similarity search to find relevant precedents.

📋 Policy Enforcement

Deterministic rules enforce vendor policies (min/max bounds, flexibility levels) to ensure decisions stay within strategic guardrails.

🔍 Reasoning with Evidence

Every recommendation shows its reasoning: policy analysis, similar past decisions, and confidence scoring based on evidence.

💾 Persistence & Learning

User decisions are stored with embeddings, immediately becoming evidence for future tender evaluations.

How It Works

  1. View Historical Proposals: See past tenders grouped by outcome (WON, LOST, REJECTED) with detailed decisions.
  2. Evaluate New Tenders: System analyzes requirements against policies and finds similar past decisions using vector search.
  3. Review Recommendations: See recommended values with reasoning bullets, confidence scores, and evidence from similar cases.
  4. Accept or Override: Users can accept recommendations or override with custom values and rationale.
  5. Persistence: Decisions are immediately stored and influence future tender evaluations.

Key Concepts

Evaluation Dimensions

  • Maintenance Duration: Years of post-delivery maintenance
  • Warranty Years: Standard warranty period
  • Payment Terms: Days until payment due
  • Local Content: Percentage of locally-sourced components

Outcome Types

  • WON: We submitted and won the contract
  • LOST: We submitted but client chose another vendor
  • REJECTED: We withdrew before submission (unacceptable terms)

Reasoning Status

  • BLOCK Cannot proceed (hard policy violation)
  • WARN Risky but possible (requires justification)
  • SAFE Recommended (within policy, positive precedent)
